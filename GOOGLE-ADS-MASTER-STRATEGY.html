1. ROI vs ROAS Definitions

Return on Investment (ROI) ROI = (Revenue - Ad Spend) / Ad Spend √ó 100% Example: $21,000 revenue - $1,000 spend = $20,000 profit ROI = $20,000 / $1,000 = 20.0 or 2,000%
Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) ROAS = Revenue / Ad Spend Example: $21,000 revenue / $1,000 spend = 21.0 ROAS
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Distinction: A 20:1 ROI means profit is 20√ó ad spend, requiring ROAS of 21.0. If the user meant ROAS 20.0, profit would be 19√ó ad spend. Throughout this plan, we assume ROI 20:1 = ROAS 21.0 (conservative interpretation).
